Full Post Text

Calling all nut lovers and nut butter lovers! A study published this month (December) in The Journal of Nutrition declares USDA scientists have found that walnuts contain 21% fewer calories than previously thought!

This means that a typical (lol) 28-gram serving actually contains 146 calories, which 21% les than the current value of 185 calories given by the USDA.

So, you may be wondering how on earth they messed up for so long (I know I was). This is actually because of the way calories were measured in the 1950s. Scientists grouped walnuts and other tree nuts with foods like dry beans, legumes and peas. For those foods, these methods work well, but when it comes to tree nuts, the values are over-estimated because the body doesn't absorb the full caloric content due to the difficult to digest fiber of the nuts.

Similar methods have found that almonds contain 20% fewer calories than previous standards, and pistachios contain 5% fewer.

The spin: Ok, so this is exciting and all, but when you think about it, this shouldn't change your diet game-plan TOO much. If you eat nuts and you're currently at your goal weight, or not actively trying to lose weight, you've been eating the RIGHT amount for your metabolism all along! Just because the papers say walnuts contain fewer calories doesn't mean that your body will magically change. Your body has known this all along!

But as for me, I may indulge in a few more walnuts and almonds this holiday season.
